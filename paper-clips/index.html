History of Paperclips

This page explores the invention, evolution, and cultural impact of the paper clip, from its early patents to its symbolic uses worldwide.

Early Inventions

The first patented bent-wire clip was granted to Samuel B. Fay in 1867. Though intended for attaching tickets, it soon became popular for bundling papers.

Gem Design

The ubiquitous Gem clip, introduced in Britain in the early 1880s, uses two loops that grip sheets together through torsion. It was never patented, but Cushman & Denison trademarked the name in 1904.

Norwegian Myth

Johan Vaaler received patents in 1899–1901 for a similar design, but his clip was less practical. Norwegian resistance fighters later adopted the clip as a symbol of unity, cementing its cultural legacy.

Other Applications

Paper clips have been repurposed for tasks like SIM‑card removal, lock‑picking, and as a quick-fix in electronics.
